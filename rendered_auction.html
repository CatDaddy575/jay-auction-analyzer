This 1979 Ford F-250 Ranger is a 4×4 pickup that had its 400ci V8 rebuilt under the original owner’s stewardship, and it was purchased from their grandson by the seller in 2025. Subsequently work included a repaint in Dark Brown Metallic, reupholstering the interior in brown, and installing a 4″ Rough Country lift kit and aftermarket 16″ eight-hole wheels. The engine features a Lunati camshaft, an Edelbrock carburetor and intake manifold, and an aftermarket aluminum radiator, and it is linked with a four-speed manual transmission and a dual-range transfer case. Further details include the Camper Special Package, the Visibility and Convenience groups, an aftermarket dual exhaust system, RetroStripeCo exterior accents, a spray-in bedliner, locking front hubs, and a Kenwood stereo. This F-250 Ranger is said to have been sold new in Washington and is now offered at no reserve with a Deluxe Marti Report, the owner’s manual, and a Washington title in the seller’s name.

The truck was repainted in March 2026, reportedly in its factory shade of Dark Brown Metallic, and RetroStripeCo two-tone accents were added at that time. Exterior details include a Styleside bed, a spray-in bedliner, a sliding rear window, a rear step bumper, swing-out side mirrors, and chrome bumpers.

The truck rides on a 4″ Rough Country lift kit, which includes shocks and leaf springs. The aftermarket 16″ eight-hole wheels are mounted with 315/75 Dick Cepek Trail Country EXP tires that were installed in May 2026. The front axle is equipped with locking hubs, and braking is provided by power-assisted front discs and rear drums. The brake master cylinder was replaced in June 2026.

The cabin was reupholstered in June 2025, and the bench seat is trimmed in brown with diamond-patterned inserts. The dash pad is color-coordinated, the door have dark brown panels, and light brown carpets line the footwells. Further interior details include a Kenwood stereo, a heater, and a defroster.

The three-spoke aftermarket steering wheel frames a column-mounted 8k-rpm tachometer, and additional instrumentation includes an 85-mph speedometer as well as gauges for coolant temperature, oil pressure, battery charge status, and fuel level. Aftermarket auxiliary gauges are mounted below the center of the dash. The five-digit odometer shows 12k miles, approximately 1k of which were added under current ownership.

The truck is powered by a 400ci V8 that is said to have been rebuilt under prior ownership. It features a Lunati camshaft, an Edelbrock carburetor and intake manifold, and an aftermarket aluminum radiator and air cleaner setup. The truck is equipped with dual fuel tanks.

Power is routed to the rear or all four wheels via a four-speed manual transmission and a dual-range transfer case. The truck has an aftermarket dual exhaust system.

The Deluxe Marti report lists a build date of March 9th, 1979, and displays production statistics, factory colors, and equipment.

The Washington title carries a “Classic” brand.
